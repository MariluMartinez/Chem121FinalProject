In this page, I discuss cerebral palsy, the different types of cerebral palsy, and treatments for people with cerebral palsy.

What is Cerebral Palsy?

Cerebral Palsy is a combination of multiple neurological disorders. It affects a person's body, and their ability to move and speak. Although there is no main cause for cerebral palsy, it can be caused by disturbances during the prenatal, perinatal, or postnatal periods. Some examples are low birth weight, premature birth, infections, or brain injury. This causes the brain involved in muscle movement to not develop properly during fetal development.

Types of Cerebral Palsy

There are four main types of Cerebral Palsy:

    Spastic Cerebral Palsy
    • Most common type
    • Main symptom is increased muscle tone, muscles are stiff
    • Three different types of Spastic CP:
      • Spastic diplegia/disparesis
        • Muscle stiffness mostly in legs
        • Tight hips
        • Legs pull inward and cross at knees
      • Spastic hemiplegia/hemiparesis
        • Affects only one side of the body, usually the arm
      • Spastic Quadriplegia/quadriparesis
        • Most severe type
        • Affects all four limbs and face
        • Can't walk or needs a device to help walk
        • Intellectual disability
        • Seizures
        • Problems with vision, hearing, and/or speech

    Dyskinetic Cerebral Palsy
    • Problems controlling movement of hands, arms, feet, legs, there is difficulty in sitting and walking
    • Movements are uncontrollable and can be slow and writhing or rapid and jerky
    • Sometimes the tongue and mouth are affected, person has a hard time sucking, swallowing, and talking
    • Symptoms change day to day or multiple times in a single day

    Ataxic Cerebral Palsy
    • Problems with balance and coordination
    • Unsteady walking
    • Hard time with quick movements or with movements that need control

    Mixed Cerebral Palsy
    • Symptoms of more than one type of CP
    • Most common mix is spastic-dyskinetic CP

People with CP can have more symptoms than just issues with their motor control function. CP is caused mainly because of brain injury, meaning injury to the developing brain. This can cause other neurological disorders to occur.

Examples include:

  • Intellectual disability
  • Seizure disorder
  • Delayed growth
  • Spinal deformities
  • Impaired vision
  • Hearing loss
  • Speech and/or language disorders
  • Excessive drooling (because people with CP do not have control of muscles in their throat, mouth, or tongue, this can lead to drooling)
  • Incontinence
  • Difficulty with sensations and perceptions
  • Malnutrition
  • Dental problems
  • Bone health
  • Psychological issues

Treatments

Cerebral Palsy doesn't worsen over time. There is no cure for it either, but with a treatment plan, the person's life can get easier or more manageable.

Potential Treatments:

  • Surgery
  • Physical therapy
  • Occupational therapy
  • Speech therapy
  • Medications
  • Mobility Devices
